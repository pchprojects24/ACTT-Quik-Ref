Adult Bradycardia with a Pulse Algorithm

Clinical Indication

Heart rate typically < 50/min.

Primary Assessment

Identify and treat underlying causes. Maintain patent airway, assist breathing as necessary, and administer oxygen if hypoxemic. Monitor ECG, blood pressure, and oximetry. Establish IV/IO access and obtain a 12-lead ECG (do not delay therapy).

Assess for Hemodynamic Instability

Are there signs of poor perfusion? (Hypotension, acutely altered mental status, signs of shock, ischemic chest discomfort, acute heart failure)

  • If NO: Monitor and observe.
  • If YES:
    • First-line: Atropine 1 mg IV. May repeat every 3 to 5 minutes up to a maximum of 3 mg.
    • If Atropine is ineffective:
      • Transcutaneous pacing.
      • OR Dopamine infusion: 5 to 20 mcg/kg/min, titrated to patient response.
      • OR Epinephrine infusion: 2 to 10 mcg/min, titrated to patient response.
    • Seek expert consultation.
Adult Tachycardia with a Pulse Algorithm

Clinical Indication

Heart rate typically > 150/min.

Primary Assessment

Maintain patent airway, assist breathing, administer oxygen if hypoxemic. Attach cardiac monitor to identify rhythm, monitor blood pressure, and oximetry.

Assess for Hemodynamic Instability

Are there signs of poor perfusion? (Hypotension, acutely altered mental status, signs of shock, ischemic chest discomfort, acute heart failure)

  • If YES (Unstable):
    • Synchronized cardioversion.
    • Consider procedural sedation (e.g., Midazolam 1 to 2.5 mg IV direct, or Ketamine 1 mg/kg IV push over 60 seconds).
    • If regular narrow complex, consider Adenosine 6 mg IV rapid push.
  • If NO (Stable):
    • Establish IV access and obtain 12-lead ECG.
    • Assess QRS Duration:
      • Wide (≥ 0.12 sec): Consider Adenosine only if regular and monomorphic. Consider antiarrhythmic infusion (e.g., Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 minutes). Consult an expert.
      • Narrow (< 0.12 sec): Perform vagal maneuvers. Administer Adenosine 6 mg IV rapid push; if no conversion, give 12 mg IV rapid push. Consider Beta-blocker or Calcium channel blocker. Consult an expert.
Electrical Cardioversion Algorithm

Clinical Indication

Tachyarrhythmias causing hemodynamic instability.

Preparation

  1. Administer sedation (e.g., Ketamine 1 mg/kg IV or Midazolam 1 to 2.5 mg IV direct) if the patient is conscious and time permits.
  2. Turn on defibrillator and attach monitoring leads/defibrillation pads to the patient.
  3. Press the SYNC button. Visually confirm that sync markers appear on the R waves.

Energy Selection (Biphasic)

  • Narrow Regular (e.g., SVT, Atrial Flutter): 50 to 100 Joules.
  • Narrow Irregular (e.g., Atrial Fibrillation): 120 to 200 Joules.
  • Wide Regular (e.g., Monomorphic VT): 100 Joules.
  • Wide Irregular (e.g., Polymorphic VT): Do not sync. Deliver unsynchronized defibrillation dose.

Execution

Announce "Clear," ensure no physical contact with the patient or bed, press and hold the "Shock" button, and immediately reassess rhythm and pulse.

Adult BLS Algorithm for Healthcare Providers

Initial Steps

  1. Verify scene safety.
  2. Check for responsiveness. If unresponsive, shout for nearby help, activate the emergency response system, and acquire an AED/defibrillator.
  3. Simultaneously look for no breathing or only gasping and check the carotid pulse for 5 to 10 seconds.

Action Matrix

  • Normal breathing, pulse present: Monitor until emergency responders arrive.
  • No normal breathing, pulse present: Provide rescue breathing (1 breath every 6 seconds). Check pulse every 2 minutes.
  • No breathing or only gasping, no pulse: Begin CPR. Cycles of 30 compressions to 2 breaths. Use the AED as soon as it is available.

AED Use

  • Shockable rhythm: Give 1 shock. Resume CPR immediately for 2 minutes until prompted by AED to allow rhythm check.
  • Non-shockable rhythm: Resume CPR immediately for 2 minutes.
Adult Cardiac Arrest Algorithm

Continuous Actions

CPR quality (100–120/min, 2–2.4 inches depth), administer oxygen, attach monitor/defibrillator.

Rhythm Check

Shockable (VF/pVT):

  1. Shock (120–200J biphasic).
  2. CPR for 2 minutes. Establish IV/IO access.
  3. Shock.
  4. CPR for 2 minutes. Administer Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO every 3 to 5 minutes. Consider advanced airway.
  5. Shock.
  6. CPR for 2 minutes. Administer Amiodarone (300 mg bolus, second dose 150 mg) OR Lidocaine (1 to 1.5 mg/kg, second dose 0.5 to 0.75 mg/kg).

Non-Shockable (Asystole/PEA):

  1. Administer Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO as soon as possible, then every 3 to 5 minutes.
  2. CPR for 2 minutes. Establish IV/IO access. Consider advanced airway.
  3. Rhythm check every 2 minutes.

Reversible Causes (H's and T's)

Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (acidosis), Hypo-/hyperkalemia, Hypothermia, Tension pneumothorax, Tamponade (cardiac), Toxins, Thrombosis (pulmonary or coronary).

Adult Post-Cardiac Arrest Rhythm Algorithm

Clinical Indication

Return of Spontaneous Circulation (ROSC).

Management Steps

  1. Manage Airway and Breathing: Place endotracheal tube if necessary. Start at 10 breaths/min. Titrate oxygen to maintain SpO2 92–98%. Titrate ventilation to maintain PaCO2 35–45 mmHg.
  2. Manage Hemodynamics: Manage hypotension (SBP < 90 mmHg or MAP < 65 mmHg).
    • Administer IV crystalloid bolus (1–2 L).
    • Initiate vasopressor infusion (Norepinephrine 2 to 4 mcg/min IV infusion, titrate to blood pressure; OR Epinephrine 2 to 10 mcg/min).
  3. Obtain 12-lead ECG.
  4. Cardiac Intervention: If STEMI is present, unstable cardiogenic shock, or mechanical circulatory support is required, transport to a PCI-capable facility immediately.
  5. Targeted Temperature Management (TTM): If the patient does not follow commands (comatose), initiate TTM between 32°C and 36°C for at least 24 hours. Obtain brain CT and monitor via EEG.
Management of Acute Cardiac Chest Pain

Algorithm Steps

  1. Assess ABCs; ensure patent airway and continuous monitoring.
  2. Administer Oxygen if SpO2 < 90%.
  3. Obtain 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of first medical contact.
  4. Administer Aspirin 160 to 325 mg chewed (unless contraindicated).
  5. Administer Nitroglycerin 0.4 mg sublingual spray every 5 minutes PRN for ischemic symptoms (maximum 3 doses). Contraindicated if SBP < 90 mmHg, suspected right ventricular infarction, or recent use of phosphodiesterase inhibitors.
  6. Administer Morphine 1 to 5 mg IV every 5 to 15 minutes PRN for ongoing ischemic symptoms unresponsive to Nitroglycerin.
  7. Classify ECG:
    • STEMI (Proceed to Fibrinolytic/PCI workflow).
    • NSTEMI/Unstable Angina.
    • Non-cardiac etiology.
Fibrinolytic Checklist

Prior to fibrinolytic therapy, verify the absence of all absolute contraindications.

Absolute Contraindications

  • Any history of intracranial hemorrhage.
  • Ischemic stroke within 3 months (EXCEPT acute ischemic stroke within 4.5 hours).
  • Known structural cerebral vascular lesion (e.g., arteriovenous malformation).
  • Known malignant intracranial neoplasm (primary or metastatic).
  • Suspected aortic dissection.
  • Active bleeding or bleeding diathesis (excluding menses).
  • Significant closed-head or facial trauma within 3 months.
  • Intracranial or intraspinal surgery within 2 months.
  • Severe uncontrolled hypertension (unresponsive to emergency therapy).

Relative Contraindications (Require clinical judgment)

  • History of chronic, severe, poorly controlled hypertension.
  • Severe, uncontrolled hypertension on presentation (SBP > 180 or DBP > 110).
  • History of prior ischemic stroke > 3 months, dementia, or known intracranial pathology not covered above.
  • Traumatic or prolonged (> 10 minutes) CPR.
  • Major surgery within 3 weeks.
  • Recent internal bleeding (within 2 to 4 weeks).
  • Noncompressible vascular punctures.
  • Active peptic ulcer.
  • Current use of anticoagulants (bleeding risk increases with elevated INR).
Regional STEMI Fibrinolytic Therapy Orders

Order Set

  • Nitroglycerin: 0.4 mg sublingual spray Q5MIN PRN for ischemic symptoms. If not resolved after 3 doses, transition to IV.
  • Nitroglycerin IV Infusion: Initiate at 10 mcg/min PRN; titrate by 10 mcg/min Q5MIN PRN (up to maximum rate 200 mcg/min). Maintain SBP above 90 mmHg (or strict drop < 30 mmHg from baseline).
  • Morphine: 1 to 5 mg IV Q5 to 15 minutes PRN for ongoing ischemic symptoms.
  • Dimenhydrinate: 25 to 50 mg IV or PO Q4H PRN for nausea.
  • Acetaminophen: 325 to 650 mg PO Q4H PRN for pain (maximum 4g/24h).
  • Lorazepam: 1 mg PO or sublingual Q4H PRN for anxiety or restlessness.
  • Electrolyte Management: Contact medical director for potassium supplementation if K+ < 3.0 mmol/L.
ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction Management

Diagnosis

12-lead ECG identifies STEMI.

Reperfusion Strategy Selection

  • Primary PCI: Preferred if time from first medical contact to device deployment is < 120 minutes. Administer ASA, P2Y12 inhibitor, and systemic anticoagulation.
  • Fibrinolysis: Preferred if time to PCI > 120 minutes and symptom onset is < 12 hours without absolute contraindications.

Execution of Fibrinolysis

  • Administer Tenecteplase (TNKase) utilizing weight-based dosing.
  • Administer adjunctive therapies (ASA, Clopidogrel, Enoxaparin or UFH).

Post-Fibrinolysis

Transfer to a PCI-capable center. Monitor for reperfusion arrhythmias and resolution of ST segments. Assess need for rescue PCI if fibrinolysis fails.

Tenecteplase (TNK) Instructions

Indication

Fibrinolysis of coronary artery thrombosis associated with STEMI.

Reconstitution

Reconstitute vial with 10 mL sterile water for injection to reach a final concentration of 5 mg/mL. Swirl gently to dissolve; DO NOT SHAKE.

Administration

Administer IV direct over 5 seconds. Flush dextrose-containing lines with NS prior to and following administration.

Weight-Based Dosing Chart

  • Less than 60 kg: 30 mg (6 mL)
  • 60 to 69 kg: 35 mg (7 mL)
  • 70 to 79 kg: 40 mg (8 mL)
  • 80 to 89 kg: 45 mg (9 mL)
  • 90 kg or more: 50 mg (10 mL)

Monitoring

Monitor strictly for bleeding, reperfusion arrhythmias, and allergic reactions (anaphylactoid reactions, hypotension). Avoid IM injections and non-compressible vascular punctures.

Drug Medications for Acute MI Management

Medication List

  • Aspirin: 160 to 325 mg chewed to inhibit platelet aggregation.
  • Nitroglycerin: 0.4 mg SL spray; IV infusion initiated at 10 mcg/min, titrated by 10 mcg/min q5min. Promotes coronary vasodilation and reduces preload.
  • Morphine: 1 to 5 mg IV Q5 to 15 minutes PRN. Provides analgesia and reduces sympathetic tone.
  • Tenecteplase (TNKase): 30 to 50 mg IV push over 5 seconds (weight-based). Primary fibrinolytic agent.
  • Dimenhydrinate: 25 to 50 mg IV max rate 25 mg/minute, for secondary nausea/vomiting.
  • Norepinephrine: 2 to 4 mcg/minute continuous IV infusion, titrated every 10 to 15 minutes to achieve desired blood pressure in the presence of cardiogenic shock.
  • Epinephrine: 1 mg IV direct Q3-5 min (for cardiac arrest secondary to MI); or 5 to 20 mcg IV direct / 1 to 20 mcg/min infusion for severe cardiac failure/hypotension.
