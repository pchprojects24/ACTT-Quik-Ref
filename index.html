### 1. ACYCLOVIR * **Classification:** Antiviral * **Indications:** * Treatment of herpes simplex encephalitis. * Severe initial clinical episodes of genital herpes. * Varicella-zoster (shingles) infections in immunocompromised patients. * **Dosage:** * **Adult:** 5 to 10 mg/kg IV every 8 hours depending on indication (e.g., 10 mg/kg for encephalitis). * **Pediatric (Secondary):** 10 to 20 mg/kg IV every 8 hours depending on age and indication. * **Preparation & Administration:** * **Reconstitution:** Add Sterile Water for Injection (SWFI) to the vial to yield a concentration of 50 mg/mL (e.g., 10 mL SWFI to a 500 mg vial). Shake well until completely dissolved. Do not use bacteriostatic water containing parabens. * **Dilution:** MUST be further diluted before administration. Dilute the reconstituted solution with a compatible IV fluid (e.g., NS, D5W, LR) to a final concentration of 7 mg/mL or less. (Example: A 500 mg dose must be diluted in at least 100 mL of fluid). * **Administration Rate:** Infuse intermittently over at least 1 hour. Rapid infusion significantly increases the risk of renal toxicity. * **Route:** Intermittent IV infusion ONLY. DO NOT administer IV direct (bolus), IM, or SC. * **Hydration:** Ensure adequate patient hydration during the infusion and for 2 hours post-infusion. * **Compatibility & Stability:** * Reconstituted vials (50 mg/mL): Stable for 12 hours at room temperature. DO NOT refrigerate (precipitation will occur). * Diluted infusion bags (≤ 7 mg/mL): Stable for 24 hours at room temperature. If visible turbidity or crystallization appears, discard. * **Hazards:** * Severe local tissue damage upon extravasation; phlebitis at the IV site. * Nephrotoxicity (crystalluria) linked to rapid infusion or inadequate hydration. * Neurotoxicity (lethargy, confusion, tremors, seizures). --- ### 2. ADENOSINE * **Classification:** Antiarrhythmic * **Indications:** * Conversion of paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia (SVT) to sinus rhythm. * Diagnostic aid for broad or narrow complex SVT. * **Dosage:** * **Adult:** Initial dose: 6 mg IV. If tachycardia does not respond within 1-2 minutes, give a second dose of 12 mg. Do not exceed 12 mg IV as a single bolus. * **Pediatric (Secondary):** Less than 50 kg: 0.05-0.1 mg/kg IV (max 6 mg). May increase by 0.05-0.1 mg/kg increments every 1-2 mins to max 0.3 mg/kg. 50 kg or greater: give adult dose. * **Preparation & Administration:** * **Preparation:** Supplied as a ready-to-use solution. Do not dilute. * **Administration Rate:** IV direct: rapid push over 1-2 seconds. * **Route:** Administer in a port as close to the trunk as possible. * **Flush:** MUST be followed immediately with a rapid NS flush (20 mL for adults, 5-10 mL for pediatrics) to ensure it reaches systemic circulation quickly due to its extremely short half-life. * **Monitoring:** Continuous cardiac monitoring required. * **Compatibility & Stability:** * Store vials and prefilled syringes at room temperature (15-30°C). Do not refrigerate as crystallization can occur. If crystals form, they will dissolve when warmed to room temperature. * **Hazards:** * Facial flushing, shortness of breath, chest pressure, transient arrhythmias (PVCs, PACs, AV block, asystole). * Bronchospasm in patients with asthma or COPD. --- ### 3. AMIODARONE * **Classification:** Antiarrhythmic * **Indications:** * Management of life-threatening recurrent ventricular fibrillation (VF) or hemodynamically unstable ventricular tachycardia (VT). * Pharmacologic cardioversion of atrial fibrillation. * **Dosage:** * **Adult (Cardiac Arrest):** 300 mg IV/IO push, followed by 150 mg if needed. * **Adult (Stable VT/Arrhythmias):** 150 mg IV over 10 minutes, followed by 1 mg/min infusion for 6 hours, then 0.5 mg/min for 18 hours. * **Pediatric (Secondary):** Cardiac arrest: 5 mg/kg rapid IV/IO (max 300 mg). Perfusing tachycardias: 5 mg/kg IV over 20-60 minutes. * **Preparation & Administration:** * **Cardiac Arrest (IV Direct):** Push undiluted or diluted in 20-30 mL D5W rapidly. * **Intermittent/Continuous Infusion:** Dilute strictly in D5W. Must be mixed in a glass or polyolefin bag (PVC bags absorb the drug and leach DEHP plasticizers). * **Filter:** An in-line 0.22-micron filter MUST be used during administration. * **Route:** Central line preferred for continuous infusions due to high risk of phlebitis. Volumetric pump required. * **Compatibility & Stability:** * Store at room temperature and protect from light. Light protection is not required during administration. * **Hazards:** * Hypotension, bradycardia, AV block, severe phlebitis, proarrhythmic effects. --- ### 4. AMPICILLIN * **Classification:** Antibiotic (Aminopenicillin) * **Indications:** * Treatment of susceptible bacterial infections including meningitis, septicemia, endocarditis, and respiratory tract infections. * **Dosage:** * **Adult:** 1 to 2 g IV every 4 to 6 hours. Up to 12 g/day for severe infections. * **Pediatric (Secondary):** 100 to 400 mg/kg/day divided every 4 to 6 hours depending on severity. * **Preparation & Administration:** * **Reconstitution:** Reconstitute with SWFI (e.g., 5 mL for a 1 g vial, 10 mL for a 2 g vial). Do not exceed a rate of 500 mg/min to prevent seizures. * **Intermittent Infusion:** Further dilute the reconstituted solution in 50-100 mL of Normal Saline (NS). Infuse over 15 to 30 minutes. * **Fluid Restriction:** Do NOT dilute in D5W as ampicillin degrades rapidly in dextrose-containing solutions. * **Compatibility & Stability:** * Rapid degradation. Use reconstituted vials within 1 hour. NS infusion bags are stable for up to 8 hours at room temperature. * **Hazards:** * Hypersensitivity reactions (anaphylaxis, rash, urticaria), GI upset, pain at the injection site. Cross-reactivity possible in patients with cephalosporin allergies. --- ### 5.
